Combat System - Antediluvian Age

Core Combat Rules

Turn Structure (5e Compatible)

Each turn in Nephilim Wars follows the standard D&D 5e action economy with antediluvian enhancements:

  1. Movement: Up to your speed (typically 30 ft)
  2. Action: Attack, Cast a Spell, Dash, Disengage, Dodge, Help, Hide, Ready, Search, Use Object
  3. Bonus Action: If you have an ability that uses a bonus action
  4. Reaction: One per round (opportunity attacks, etc.)
  5. Free Actions: Drop item, short communication, etc.

Initiative

Roll: 1d20 + DEX modifier

Antediluvian Variant - Spiritual Awareness:

  • If your RP ≥ 10: Add +2 to initiative
  • If your CP ≥ 10: Subtract -2 from initiative
  • Angels and righteous beings always act before corrupted creatures on tied initiative

Attack Resolution

Melee Attacks ▶

Attack Roll: 1d20 + STR modifier + proficiency bonus (if proficient)

Hit if: Attack roll ≥ target's AC

Critical Hit: Natural 20 - roll all damage dice twice

Critical Miss: Natural 1 - automatic miss (optional fumble table)

Damage: Weapon damage + STR modifier

Advantage/Disadvantage

  • Advantage: Roll 2d20, take higher (flanking, prone target, etc.)
  • Disadvantage: Roll 2d20, take lower (restrained, prone attacker, etc.)
Ranged Attacks ▶

Attack Roll: 1d20 + DEX modifier + proficiency bonus

Range: Most weapons have normal/long range (e.g., 80/320)

  • Normal range: No penalty
  • Long range: Disadvantage on attack
  • Beyond long range: Cannot attack

Cover:

  • Half cover: +2 AC and DEX saves
  • Three-quarters cover: +5 AC and DEX saves
  • Full cover: Cannot be targeted directly

Adjacent Enemies: Disadvantage on ranged attacks if hostile creature within 5 ft

Damage & Hit Points

Taking Damage: Reduce your HP by the damage amount

Bloodied: When you reach half HP or less, you can use Desperation Actions

Reaching 0 HP: You fall unconscious and begin making death saves (or use Grievous Wounds variant)

Death Saves

  • Roll 1d20 at the start of your turn
  • 10+: Success (record one success)
  • 1-9: Failure (record one failure)
  • Natural 20: Regain 1 HP and become conscious
  • Natural 1: Count as two failures
  • Three successes: Stabilized (unconscious but alive)
  • Three failures: Dead
Antediluvian Mercy: Allies can use an Action to stabilize you with DC 10 Medicine check, or spend 1 RP to automatically stabilize you from 30 ft away.

Righteousness & Corruption System

Righteousness Points (RP)

RP represents your spiritual alignment with divine order, accumulated through righteous acts, prayer, and moral choices.

Gaining RP

  • Protecting the innocent: +1-3 RP
  • Resisting temptation: +1-2 RP
  • Acts of mercy: +1 RP
  • Prayer and worship: +1 RP per hour of sincere prayer
  • Destroying corrupted artifacts: +2-5 RP
  • Confession and repentance: Variable, removes CP

RP Combat Benefits

Cost Benefit
1 RP Reroll any d20 (attack, save, ability check)
2 RP Grant ally within 30 ft advantage on next roll
3 RP Automatically stabilize dying ally within 30 ft
5 RP Invoke Divine Shield - gain resistance to next attack
7 RP Righteous Smite - add 3d8 radiant damage to attack

Passive RP Bonuses

  • RP 5+: +1 to WIS saving throws
  • RP 10+: +1 to AC and all saves, +2 initiative
  • RP 15+: Advantage on saves against fear and charm
  • RP 20+: Faint golden aura, disadvantage on Stealth but advantage on Intimidation vs corrupted

Corruption Points (CP)

CP represents the taint of the fallen world, gained through sinful acts, exposure to darkness, and embracing forbidden power.

Gaining CP

  • Killing innocents: +3-5 CP
  • Using forbidden knowledge: +1-3 CP
  • Consorting with demons: +2 CP
  • Breaking sacred oaths: +3 CP
  • Accepting dark bargains: +1-5 CP
  • Consuming corrupted flesh: +2 CP
  • Witnessing cosmic horrors: +1-3 CP

CP Combat Consequences

CP/RP Balance & Redemption

Removing Corruption

  • Prayer & Fasting: Spend 8 hours in prayer, remove 1 CP (once per week)
  • Confession: Confess sins to righteous priest/elder, DC 15 Religion check, remove 1d4 CP
  • Sacrifice: Sacrifice something precious (item worth 100+ gp or personal sacrifice), remove 1d6 CP
  • Divine Intervention: Angel or prophet can remove CP (rare, quest reward)
  • Righteous Acts: For every 3 RP gained, may spend those RP to remove 1 CP

Point of No Return

⚠️ CP 25+: Character is at risk of full corruption. Must make DC 18 WIS save every long rest or become NPC under GM control as a fallen, corrupted being. Only divine miracle can save them now.

Spiritual Warfare System

Spiritual Integrity (SI)

SI = 10 + WIS modifier + character level

SI represents your soul's resilience against supernatural assault. While HP measures physical vitality, SI measures spiritual wholeness.

What Damages SI?

  • Angel weapons (radiant damage to corrupted beings)
  • Demon weapons (necrotic/psychic damage to righteous beings)
  • Demonic whispers and temptations
  • Ancient curses and hexes
  • Witnessing cosmic horrors
  • Exposure to fallen watcher magic
  • Certain forbidden rituals

Spiritual Crisis (SI Reaches 0)

When your SI is reduced to 0, you don't die - but your soul enters crisis. Roll 1d10 on the Spiritual Crisis table:

d10 Crisis Effect Duration
1-2 Despair: Disadvantage on all saves, cannot spend RP Until SI restored to 3+
3-4 Possession Vulnerability: Demons have advantage on possession attempts against you Until SI restored to 5+
5-6 Divine Abandonment: Cannot pray for RP, -2 to WIS-based checks 24 hours or until confession
7-8 Corruption Surge: Gain 1d6 CP immediately, nightmarish visions Permanent (until CP removed)
9 Soul Fracture: Max HP reduced by 10, visible spiritual wound Until Greater Restoration or divine healing
10 Marked by Darkness: Demons can sense you from miles away, gain 2d4 CP Permanent scar (quest to remove)
Critical: Multiple Spiritual Crises compound. If SI reaches 0 while already in crisis, roll again and suffer both effects simultaneously.

SI Recovery

Natural Recovery

  • Long Rest: Restore 1 SI
  • Prayer (1 hour): Restore 1d4 SI (once per long rest)
  • Sabbath Rest: Full 24-hour rest restores all SI

Active Restoration

  • Confession: DC 12 Religion check, restore 1d6 SI
  • Divine Blessing: Priest/prophet can restore 2d8 SI (requires ritual)
  • Sacred Site: Resting at altar/holy place restores 1d4 extra SI
  • Angelic Touch: Angel can fully restore SI (rare)

RP-Based SI Healing

Spend 2 RP as an action to restore 1d6 SI to yourself or an ally you touch.

Making Spiritual Attacks ▶

When to Target SI Instead of HP

Certain beings and weapons specifically assault the soul:

  • Angels wielding holy weapons against the corrupted
  • Demons using psychic/necrotic powers
  • Watcher magic and forbidden spells
  • Cursed artifacts
  • Eldritch phenomena

Spiritual Attack Roll

Attacker rolls: 1d20 + CHA modifier + proficiency (for most spiritual attacks)

Defender's Spiritual Defense = 10 + WIS modifier + level/2

On Hit

Roll SI damage as specified by the attack. Target's current SI is reduced.

Critical Spiritual Hits (Natural 20)

Double SI damage dice AND target must make WIS save (DC = 8 + attacker's proficiency + CHA mod) or suffer additional effect:

  • Righteous crit on corrupted: Target gains 1d4 CP and is frightened 1 round
  • Corrupted crit on righteous: Target loses 1 RP and disadvantage on next save
Dual Damage: Some powerful attacks deal BOTH HP and SI damage. For example, a fallen angel's sword might deal 2d8 slashing (HP) + 1d6 necrotic (SI).

Giants were legendary wrestlers. Nephilim Wars makes grappling dynamic and tactical.

Initiating Grapple

Action: Use Attack action, one attack

Contest: Your Athletics (STR) vs target's Athletics (STR) or Acrobatics (DEX)

Size Requirement: Target must be no more than one size larger than you

Success Levels

Beat Target By Result Effect

Action: Attempt to escape

Contest: Your Athletics or Acrobatics vs grappler's Athletics

While grappling, your speed is halved unless target is two sizes smaller. You can drag/carry the grappled creature with you.

If you have giant ancestry:

Fighting Giants (Smaller Creature Tactics)

When fighting larger foes, smaller creatures can:

  • Target Weak Points: Called shots against legs/joints (standard called shot rules)
  • Climb: Use Athletics to climb onto larger creature (contested), gain advantage on attacks while mounted
  • Evasion: Squeeze into spaces giant cannot reach (avoid attacks)
  • Trip: Coordinated attacks on legs (2+ creatures), giant makes DEX save or falls prone

Giant Bloodlines

Overview: Children of the Watchers

When the Watchers (fallen angels) took human wives, their offspring were the Nephilim - giants of great power and terrible corruption. Different bloodlines emerged, each carrying unique traits from their angelic progenitors.

Bloodline Intensity: Characters with giant heritage have varying degrees of angelic ancestry:

  • Pure-Blood Nephilim (First Generation): Direct offspring of Watcher + human
  • Rephaim/Anakim (Second-Third Generation): Grandchildren of Watchers

Interbreeding & Hybrid Bloodlines

Creating Hybrid Characters ▶

Some characters may have mixed giant heritage (e.g., Nephilim/Rephaim or Anakim/Avvim). Work with GM to create hybrid:

Hybrid Rules:

  • Choose primary bloodline (determines size, starting CP, main features)
  • Select 2-3 features from secondary bloodline
  • Average the starting CP of both bloodlines (round up)
  • May have conflicting powers (some require high CP, others require low CP)
  • Often socially outcast even among giants

Example: Nephilim/Avvim Hybrid "The Struggling Giant"

  • Primary: Nephilim (Large, powerful)
  • Secondary: Avvim (human-like features, less cursed)
  • Starting CP: 1 (average of 2 and 0)
  • Features: Nephilim Resilience, Giant's Might, Human Guise (from Avvim), Blessing of Reduction (from Avvim)
  • Roleplay: Torn between monstrous heritage and desire for redemption

Roleplaying Giant Heritage

Social Dynamics

  • Among Humans: Feared, revered, or hunted depending on actions and appearance
  • Among Other Giants: Status determined by lineage purity and strength
  • Angels' View: Abominations to be destroyed OR pitiable creatures to be redeemed (depends on angel)
  • Demons' View: Tools to be used, corrupted kin to recruit, or threats to subjugate

Internal Struggle

  • Constant battle between human morality and angelic pride
  • Temptation to use immense power for selfish ends
  • Hunger for dominance vs desire for acceptance
  • Memory of glory (if older) vs reality of being hunted
  • Question of whether redemption is even possible

Questions to Ask Your Character

  • Do you see yourself as human or giant?
  • Do you resent or embrace your heritage?
  • Have you met your Watcher ancestor? What was it like?
  • How do you view "normal" humans - as equals, prey, or charges to protect?
  • What would you sacrifice to be free of corruption?

Mass Combat Round Structure

Each mass combat round = ~10 minutes of battle time.

Round Sequence:

  1. Initiative: Each side rolls 1d20 + commander's CHA modifier
  2. Player Actions: PCs can take individual actions (see below)
  3. Unit Attacks: Each unit attacks an enemy unit
    • Roll unit's Attack vs enemy unit's Defense
    • On hit: Deal damage = 1d20 casualties
    • Reduce enemy unit size by casualties dealt
  4. Morale Checks: Units that lost 25%+ size this round make morale check
    • Roll 1d20 + current Morale score
    • DC = 10 + (casualties taken this round / 10)
    • Failure: Unit routes (flees battlefield)
  5. Assess Victory: If one side has no units remaining or all units routed, battle ends

Siege Warfare

Defending a City

City Defenses:

  • Walls: HP based on construction (stone: 500 HP, wood: 200 HP, brick: 300 HP)
  • Gates: HP = Wall HP / 5, but AC is lower (easier to damage)
  • Towers: Allow archers +2 to attack, +100 ft range
  • Moat: Prevents direct assault, must be crossed (takes 1 round, vulnerable during crossing)

Defenders Inside City:

  • +4 Defense (protected by walls)
  • +3 Morale (defending home)
  • Archers can attack from walls without being attacked by melee
  • Can sally forth (leave city to attack) but lose bonuses

Assaulting a City

Direct Assault:

  • Attacking units must first damage walls/gates to HP 0
  • While attacking walls, take damage from defender archers each round
  • Once breach made, units can enter city (normal combat)
  • Morale -2 (dangerous assault)

Starvation Siege

Surround city, cut off supplies:

  • Each week, besieged city makes CON save DC 10 (+1 per week)
  • Failure: Lose 10% of population, all units -1 morale
  • After 4 weeks: Disease may break out (GM rolls, -2d10 casualties per week)
  • City surrenders when morale drops below 5 or population drops below 25%

Special Conditions & Events

Divine Intervention in Battle ▶

Angelic Host Arrives

Trigger: Righteous army facing overwhelming evil, player calls for aid with RP 15+

Effect:

  • 1d6 Angels descend to battlefield
  • Each Angel counts as Elite unit with Size 10 (but each = 20 soldiers)
  • All corrupted units must make morale check DC 18 or route immediately
  • Righteous units gain +4 morale for remainder of battle
  • Angels specifically target giants, demons, corrupted champions

Demonic Legion Summoned

Trigger: Corrupted leader sacrifices 100+ innocents or calls on Fallen Watcher

Effect:

  • 2d10 Lesser Demons or 1d4 Greater Demons appear
  • All righteous units must make morale check DC 16 or be frightened (disadvantage on attacks)
  • Demons fight for corrupted side but may demand price after battle
  • All participants on corrupted side gain 1d4 CP
  • Sacred ground becomes defiled

Victory & Aftermath

Determining Victor

Battle ends when:

  • One side has no units remaining on field
  • One side's units all route (morale collapse)
  • Commander killed and heir routes
  • Objective achieved (city taken, artifact secured, etc.)

Spoils of War

Victory Type Spoils
Pyrrhic (Lost 60%+ of army) 500 gp × army size modifier, -2 morale for all surviving units for 1 month, enemy escapes mostly intact
Marginal (Lost 30-60%) 1,000 gp × modifier, enemy wounded leaders captured, control battlefield
Decisive (Lost <30%) 3,000 gp × modifier, enemy leaders captured/killed, weapons/armor looted, +1 morale to all units for 1 month
Total (Lost <10%) 5,000 gp × modifier, enemy annihilated, legendary victory (+2 morale permanently), songs sung of triumph, recruit new units easily

Army Size Modifier: Small (500-) = 1×, Medium (500-2000) = 2×, Large (2000-5000) = 3×, Massive (5000+) = 5×

Casualties & Recovery

  • Dead: 50% of casualties are dead (can't be recovered)
  • Wounded: 50% of casualties are wounded (recover in 1d4 weeks)
  • Routing Units: 50% return after battle (scattered, regrouped)
  • Morale Impact: Losing battle: -1d4 morale for all units. Winning battle: +1d4 morale.

Sample Battle Scenario

The Siege of Enoch's Gate

Situation: City of Enoch (righteous settlement, pop 3,000) is besieged by Nephilim warlord Azazel with army of 2,000 corrupted warriors and 50 giants.

Defender Forces (Inside City):

  • 4 units Trained Infantry (400 soldiers, +5 atk, 14 def, 12 morale + 3 city = 15)
  • 2 units Archers (200 soldiers, +5 atk, 12 def, 10 morale +3 city = 13)
  • 1 unit Veteran Temple Guard (100 elite, +7 atk, 16 def, 15 morale +3 city = 18)
  • City Walls: 500 HP, AC 15
  • Commander: Prophet Jared (CHA +4, RP 18)

Attacker Forces (Siege Lines):

  • 10 units Corrupted Warriors (1,000 soldiers, +3 atk, 10 def, 5 morale - fearless)
  • 5 units Nephilim Warband (50 giants, +12 atk, 18 def, 18 morale, terror)
  • 2 Catapults, 1 Battering Ram
  • Commander: Azazel the Warlord (Nephilim, CP 20, STR +8)

Round 1:

  • Initiative: Defenders win (Jared CHA +4)
  • PC Action: Cleric prays (spends 3 RP) - all defender units +2 attack/morale for 3 rounds
  • Catapults fire at walls: 2× 4d10 damage = 48 HP to walls (452 HP remain)
  • Defender archers fire: Deal 2d20 casualties to Corrupted Warriors (unit 1 loses 23 soldiers, now 77)
  • No morale checks (casualties <25%)

Outcome (after 8 rounds): Walls breached, desperate melee in streets, PCs kill Azazel in heroic duel. Corrupted army routes. Pyrrhic victory - city saved but 40% casualties. Prophet Jared grants blessing (all PCs +1d4 RP).
