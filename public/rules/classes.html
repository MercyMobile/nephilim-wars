‚öîÔ∏è Combat Fundamentals

Understanding Damage Types

  • Slashing: Swords, axes - Effective against unarmored foes and flesh
  • Piercing: Spears, arrows - Can penetrate armor gaps
  • Bludgeoning: Hammers, maces - Effective against armored and skeletal enemies
  • Elemental: Fire, cold, lightning - Magical damage types bypass physical armor
  • Divine: Radiant, thunder - Holy damage effective against corrupt beings

Combat Modifiers

  • Advantage: Roll twice, take higher result (roughly +5 bonus)
  • Disadvantage: Roll twice, take lower result (roughly -5 penalty)
  • Cover: Half cover (+2 AC), Three-quarters (+5 AC), Full (cannot be targeted)
  • Flanking: Gain advantage when allies surround an enemy
  • High Ground: Advantage on ranged attacks from elevated position

Soul Economy System

  • Righteousness Points (RP): Gained through noble deeds, powers divine abilities
  • Corruption Points (CP): Gained through dark magic and evil acts, empowers forbidden powers
  • Balance: Too much corruption risks losing your character to darkness
  • Class Impact: Priests thrive on RP, Magi may embrace CP for power

Action Economy

  • Action: Attack, cast spell, dash, disengage, dodge, help, or use object
  • Bonus Action: Quick actions like off-hand attacks or short spells
  • Reaction: Opportunity attacks when enemies leave your reach
  • Movement: Can be split before and after your action

Class Selection Tips

  • New Players: Warrior or Hunter - straightforward and forgiving
  • Damage Dealers: Gibbor or Magi - maximum damage output
  • Support Role: Priest - healing and buffs keep the party alive
  • Versatility: Artisan or Hunter - adapt to different situations
  • Strategic Play: Scribe or Magi - intelligence-based problem solving
